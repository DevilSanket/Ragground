## Key Points

- Miso is categorized by color and flavor; lighter means milder/sweeter, darker means stronger.
- Longer fermentation leads to darker color and stronger flavor.
- Light miso is mild, slightly sweet, and versatile for dressings and soups.
- Mild miso is saltier, more umami, and considered all-purpose.
- Dark miso is fermented for at least one year, super strong, high in umami, and pairs well with mushrooms, red meat, and stews.

## Main Takeaway

> Understanding the characteristics of light, mild, and dark miso allows you to choose the right type for different culinary applications, enhancing dishes with specific flavor profiles and umami levels.
