[informational] Types of Miso

Welcome back to Ground Up Explains. Today, let's talk about types of miso. Miso is generally categorized by its color and flavor. The lighter the color, the milder and sweeter the taste. As the shade gets darker, the flavor gets stronger. And the longer the ferment, the darker the color. So here's the light miso. If you're new to miso, this is the one I would begin with. It's mild, slightly sweet, and super versatile. I would put this in solid dressings and soops. So next up is the mild one. This one is saltier and definitely more umami. I call it an all-purpose miso. In fact, I'll even go ahead and put it in a bowl of miso. So the possibilities here are endless. Lastly, we have the dark miso. This is usually fermented minimum one year. I would describe it as super strong, but in a good way. Umami, like at the highest level, and pretty deep and bold. This would go really well with mushrooms, red meat, and even in stews. At ground up, we have multiple versions of these three types of miso.
